About This Treatment

When the soft inner pulp of a tooth becomes infected or inflamed, a root canal removes the infected tissue, cleans the canals, and seals the tooth to prevent further infection — saving the natural tooth.

Expert doctors with years of experience
Painless procedures with local anaesthesia
Modern equipment & sterile environment

Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. 1Dental X-ray taken to assess the extent of infection in the roots
  2. 2Local anesthesia is administered to ensure complete numbness
  3. 3A rubber dam is placed to keep the area clean and dry
  4. 4A small opening is drilled through the top (crown) of the tooth
  5. 5Infected pulp tissue is carefully removed using specialized files
  6. 6Each root canal is precisely shaped, cleaned, and disinfected
  7. 7The canals are filled with a biocompatible material (gutta-percha)
  8. 8The tooth is sealed with a temporary or permanent filling
  9. 9A dental crown is typically recommended to protect the treated tooth
  10. 10Follow-up X-ray to confirm successful treatment
